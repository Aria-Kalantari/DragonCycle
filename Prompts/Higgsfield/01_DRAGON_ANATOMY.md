# Prompt — Prime Adult Dragon anatomy lock

Create a natural-history creature design sheet for a **prime adult six-limbed dragon**: four weight-bearing legs and two independent wings attached to a reinforced shoulder girdle behind the forelimbs. Deep chest for flight musculature, long counterbalancing tail, flexible but protected neck, broad jaw, keratin horns shaped by wear rather than decoration. The animal is an adaptable apex grappler capable of stable quadrupedal movement and short explosive takeoff.

Show consistent front, side, top, and three-quarter views at the same scale, neutral standing pose, wings fully spread in one view and correctly folded in another. Include a 2-meter human silhouette and a 10-meter scale bar. Neutral studio-natural-history lighting, pale background, high anatomical readability, restrained earthy coloration, membrane veins and realistic joint landmarks.

Negative constraints: no extra limbs, no wing-arms replacing forelegs, no humanoid hands, no saddle, no armor, no rider, no text labels except scale markers, no cropped wings, no weightless pose, no changing horn or tail shape between views.
