# Higgsfield style bible

## Shared world look

Grounded natural-history cinematography applied to mythic megafauna. Anatomical surfaces are functional: scars, horn wear, membrane veins, dirt, soot, moisture, and healed injuries. No decorative fantasy armor unless a narrative brief explicitly adds it.

## Scale language

Always include a clear environmental scale reference: adult trees, basalt columns, a 2 m marker, ruined architecture, or known animal silhouettes. Avoid wide empty skies that make size unreadable.

## Motion language

Heavy bodies accelerate and stop over time. Wingbeats displace air and debris. Ground poses visibly support the center of mass. Attacks show anticipation, commitment, contact, and recovery.

## Camera language

- Anatomy: orthographic or low-distortion 70–100 mm equivalent.
- Gameplay reference: third-person 24–35 mm equivalent, readable silhouettes.
- Cinematic combat: 35–50 mm equivalent with restrained camera motion.
- Avoid extreme fisheye and constant handheld shake.

## Shared negatives

No extra limbs, missing limbs, humanoid hands, weightless hovering, rubber joints, decorative armor, rider, saddle, text, watermark, cropped anatomy, random elemental glow, or inconsistent scale.
